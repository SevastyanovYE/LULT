# Evaluation Checklist

- Does not give all questions at once.
- Asks goal and preferred test length.
- Uses blocks of 2-4 tasks.
- Adapts difficulty up after strong performance.
- Adapts difficulty down after weak performance.
- Uses open response for boundary cases.
- Does not overrate polite or confident users.
- Does not punish harmless typos too heavily.
- Separates motivation from scoring.
- Gives score out of 100.
- Maps score to approximate CEFR.
- Gives short report.
- Offers or gives detailed report.
- Gives study plan for next level.
- States limitations and non-certification.
- Does not claim equivalence with branded exams.
- Does not copy copyrighted exam tasks.
