# CEFR Level Descriptors

Retrieval keywords: CEFR descriptors, Pre-A1, A1, A2, B1, B2, C1, C2, level descriptors, vocabulary, grammar, reading, writing, speaking, listening.

These descriptors are original practical summaries. They are not official CEFR wording.

## Pre-A1

- Overall ability: Recognizes isolated words and memorized phrases.
- Vocabulary: Very small set: names, places, numbers, greetings.
- Grammar/control: Fragments; little sentence control.
- Reading: Understands labels, icons, very familiar words.
- Writing: Can copy or write simple personal details.
- Speaking: Can answer with single words if supported.
- Listening: Recognizes slow familiar words with repetition.

## A1

- Overall ability: Handles basic personal information and routine phrases.
- Vocabulary: Everyday topics: family, food, time, home, simple needs.
- Grammar/control: Simple present, be/have, basic word order; frequent errors.
- Reading: Understands short simple messages and forms.
- Writing: Writes short sentences about self and routine.
- Speaking: Uses memorized patterns; needs patient listener.
- Listening: Understands slow clear speech on familiar topics.

## A2

- Overall ability: Manages simple routine exchanges and concrete needs.
- Vocabulary: Wider everyday vocabulary, basic opinions, travel, shopping, work.
- Grammar/control: Simple past/future, comparatives, basic connectors; errors common but meaning often clear.
- Reading: Understands short notices, simple emails, and predictable texts.
- Writing: Writes simple paragraphs with limited linking.
- Speaking: Can describe routines, plans, likes, and past events simply.
- Listening: Understands short clear messages on common situations.

## B1

- Overall ability: Communicates on familiar topics with enough independence.
- Vocabulary: Can discuss experiences, plans, problems, and reasons.
- Grammar/control: Uses common tenses and clauses; errors appear under complexity.
- Reading: Understands straightforward articles, messages, and opinions.
- Writing: Produces connected paragraphs with reasons and examples.
- Speaking: Maintains conversation on familiar topics, sometimes searches for words.
- Listening: Follows clear standard speech on familiar matters.

## B2

- Overall ability: Communicates effectively on many concrete and some abstract topics.
- Vocabulary: Good range with some precision; can paraphrase.
- Grammar/control: Varied structures; errors rarely block meaning.
- Reading: Understands articles, arguments, and implied stance with effort.
- Writing: Develops clear arguments, comparisons, and explanations.
- Speaking: Interacts naturally with occasional hesitation or imprecision.
- Listening: Follows extended speech when structure is clear.

## C1

- Overall ability: Communicates flexibly, precisely, and with strong control.
- Vocabulary: Broad range; handles nuance, collocation, and register.
- Grammar/control: Complex structures mostly accurate; self-correction is effective.
- Reading: Understands complex texts, tone, implication, and argument structure.
- Writing: Produces well-organized, nuanced text for purpose and audience.
- Speaking: Fluent and coherent; handles abstract topics and repairs smoothly.
- Listening: Understands extended speech, implicit meaning, and varied accents.

## C2

- Overall ability: Communicates with near-native flexibility across demanding contexts.
- Vocabulary: Very broad, idiomatic, precise, and style-aware.
- Grammar/control: Highly accurate control even in complex expression.
- Reading: Understands dense, subtle, specialized, or literary texts.
- Writing: Produces polished text with rhetorical control.
- Speaking: Speaks fluently with fine nuance and natural interaction.
- Listening: Understands fast, idiomatic, and structurally complex speech.
