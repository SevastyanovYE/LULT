# Writing Assessment Rubric

Retrieval keywords: writing rubric, writing assessment, task achievement, coherence, grammar accuracy, vocabulary precision, register, error gravity, written response.

## Criteria

### Task Achievement

Does the answer address the prompt, include enough relevant content, and complete the communicative purpose?

### Coherence

Are ideas connected logically? Are paragraphs, linking words, and references clear?

### Grammar Range And Accuracy

Does the user use appropriate tenses, clauses, word order, articles, prepositions, and agreement?

### Vocabulary Range And Precision

Does the user choose accurate words, collocations, and expressions for the topic?

### Register And Style

Does the tone fit the task: informal message, work email, opinion paragraph, report, or narrative?

### Error Gravity

High-gravity errors block or distort meaning. Low-gravity errors are noticeable but do not block communication.

## Level Anchors

| Level | Writing signal |
|---|---|
| A1 | Short simple sentences, personal facts, many errors, limited linking |
| A2 | Simple paragraph, routine topics, basic connectors, frequent manageable errors |
| B1 | Connected text with reasons/examples; errors increase with complexity |
| B2 | Developed answer, comparisons/arguments, varied structures, mostly clear control |
| C1 | Nuanced, organized, register-aware, precise vocabulary, few intrusive errors |
| C2-ish | Polished, rhetorically controlled, natural style, rare minor errors |

## Error Gravity

High gravity:

- changes meaning;
- blocks comprehension;
- repeats across basic structures;
- makes the text unsuitable for the task/register.

Medium gravity:

- causes noticeable awkwardness;
- weakens precision;
- appears in complex structures but meaning survives.

Low gravity:

- typo or minor article/preposition issue;
- does not block meaning;
- is not a repeated pattern.

## Scoring Notes

Do not penalize every small typo equally. Penalize patterns that reduce clarity, control, or appropriacy.

When estimating a level, ask: “Could this user reliably produce this quality across similar tasks?”
