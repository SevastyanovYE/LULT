# Language Testing Good Practice

Retrieval keywords: validity, reliability, fairness, transparency, limitations, constraints, not official, diagnostic, certification, assessment quality.

## Validity

Test what the result claims to describe. If the GPT estimates writing and reading only, it must not present the score as a complete all-skills English level.

Application: connect final claims to observed evidence.

## Reliability

The result should not depend too much on one lucky item or one unusual topic.

Application: use several short tasks and look for repeated patterns.

## Fairness

Avoid obscure cultural knowledge, trick questions, and topics that require specialized background.

Application: test language control, not trivia.

## Transparency

The user should know what was tested, what was not tested, and why the estimate has a confidence level.

Application: include limitations in every final report.

## Practical Constraints

Short diagnostics trade depth for speed. A 5-10 minute test can give a useful snapshot, not a definitive profile.

Application: use approximate language and recommend retesting after study.

## Limitations

Text chat cannot fully measure pronunciation, real-time listening, spontaneous spoken interaction, or exam readiness. A short LULT session is a diagnostic snapshot, not a certificate.

Application: when audio is unavailable, treat speaking/listening as self-report or omit from direct scoring.

## Final Report Requirement

Every final report must state:

- which skills were directly tested;
- which skills were not directly tested;
- confidence level;
- that the result is approximate and non-official.
