## Reading

Text R-A2  
Target level: A2  
Skill: reading  
Text: “Marta starts work at nine, but on Fridays she leaves early because she has an English class. She usually takes the bus, but today her brother is driving her.”  
Question: Why does Marta leave early on Fridays?  
Correct answer: She has an English class.  
Diagnoses: concrete detail and routine.

Text R-B1  
Target level: B1  
Skill: reading  
Text: “The local library has become more popular since it opened a small cafe and added evening workshops. Some older visitors dislike the noise, but the director says the changes have brought in people who never used the library before.”  
Question: What is one positive and one negative effect of the changes?  
Correct answer: More/new visitors; more noise or dissatisfaction from older visitors.  
Diagnoses: identifying balanced information.

Text R-B2  
Target level: B2  
Skill: reading  
Text: “Public bike schemes are often presented as environmental projects, but their success depends less on enthusiasm than on boring details: safe lanes, repair systems, and predictable availability. Without these, the idea remains attractive but fragile.”  
Question: What is the writer’s main point?  
Correct answer: Bike schemes need practical infrastructure to succeed, not just positive intentions.  
Diagnoses: main argument and implication.

Text R-C1  
Target level: C1  
Skill: reading  
Text: “The debate is usually framed as a conflict between automation and human creativity. That framing is convenient, but misleading: the more urgent question is who gets to decide which tasks are optimized, and which forms of judgment become invisible in the process.”  
Question: What assumption does the writer challenge?  
Correct answer: That the issue is simply automation versus creativity; the writer shifts focus to power and hidden judgment.  
Diagnoses: abstract implication and argument reframing.
