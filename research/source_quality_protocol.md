# Source Quality Protocol

This protocol is compatible with the Evidence Retrieval Protocol v1.1: domain triage, evidence hierarchy, quality screening, conflict checks, synthesis with uncertainty, and trust scoring.

## Preferred Evidence

Start with:

1. Official standards and technical manuals.
2. Umbrella reviews, if available.
3. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
4. Peer-reviewed primary studies.
5. Expert guidance only when higher-tier evidence is unavailable.

## Avoid Or Down-Weight

- Blog posts without sources.
- Marketing pages.
- Vendor whitepapers with no independent confirmation.
- Unsourced claims about CEFR equivalence.
- Materials with fabricated, unverifiable, or mismatched references.

## Venue Checks

Prefer sources with:

- transparent authorship;
- clear methods or institutional authority;
- peer review or official publication process;
- corrections/retractions policy where relevant;
- stable URLs or bibliographic records.

## Conflict Of Interest

Mark source as industry-linked if an exam provider, course vendor, or product seller benefits from the conclusion. Do not automatically exclude it, but look for independent confirmation.

## Evidence Labels

Use these labels in notes:

- Solid: strong source quality and consistent support.
- Plausible: reasonable support but limited direct evidence.
- Uncertain: mixed evidence, weak methods, or poor fit.
- Hypothesis: useful idea not yet established.

## Reporting

Separate:

- what seems solid;
- what is uncertain;
- what is unknown;
- what should not be claimed in GPT reports.
