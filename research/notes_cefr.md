# Notes: CEFR

## Key Sources

- Council of Europe CEFR Companion Volume, 2020.
- Council of Europe book page for metadata.
- ALTE Manual for Language Test Development and Examining, 2011.
- ACTFL Proficiency Guidelines 2024 as a comparison framework, not a CEFR source.

## What Seems Solid

- CEFR is a descriptive reference framework, not a single test.
- The 2020 Companion Volume adds Pre-A1 and expands descriptors, including mediation, online interaction, plurilingual/pluricultural competence, and phonological competence.
- CEFR profiles can be uneven across skills. A learner may be stronger in reading/writing than speaking/listening.
- Linking a test to CEFR requires a validity argument and context-specific justification; using CEFR labels informally is not the same as formal alignment.

## Practical Notes For LULT

- Use CEFR as an approximate interpretive language, not as certification.
- Report skill evidence separately before giving a global snapshot.
- Use “C2-ish” or “advanced C1/C2 border” carefully; short text-chat diagnostics cannot confirm full C2.
- If speaking/listening are not directly tested, say the estimate is text-weighted.

## Claims To Avoid

- “This GPT certifies your CEFR level.”
- “This GPT is aligned to CEFR in the formal testing sense.”
- “A 0-100 LULT score is equivalent to official CEFR cut scores.”
- “A short chat can fully measure all CEFR activities.”
