## Source Quality Legend

- Tier A: official standard, official manual, systematic/scoping review, major handbook chapter.
- Tier B: peer-reviewed review, peer-reviewed theoretical or methodological article.
- Tier C: primary empirical study or technical report.
- Tier D: blog, marketing page, informal source.

## Reviews, Handbook Chapters, And Peer-Reviewed Research

### Wind, S. A., & Peterson, M. E. (2018). A systematic review of methods for evaluating rating quality in language assessment.

- Journal: Language Testing, 35(2).
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1177/0265532216686999
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0265532216686999
- Tier: A/B
- Trust notes: Systematic review of 259 studies on rating quality in rater-mediated language assessment.
- LULT use: writing/speaking scoring reliability, need for calibration examples and consistency checks.

### Fan, J., & Yan, X. (2020). Assessing Speaking Proficiency: A Narrative Review of Speaking Assessment Research Within the Argument-Based Validation Framework.

- Journal: Frontiers in Psychology, 11, 330.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2020.00330
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7057184/
- Tier: B
- Trust notes: Narrative review of empirical speaking assessment research in Language Testing and Language Assessment Quarterly.
- LULT use: speaking construct complexity, task design, rater effects, no-audio limitations.

### Davey, T., Pitoniak, M., & Slater, S. C. (2016). Designing Computerized Adaptive Tests.

- In: Handbook of Test Development, Second Edition.
- URL: https://www.ets.org/research/policy_research_reports/publications/chapter/2016/jzgc.html
- Tier: A/B
- Trust notes: ETS chapter metadata; covers CAT, IRT, item pools, item calibration, pretesting, scoring, administration, test security.
- LULT use: distinguish heuristic adaptation from formal CAT.

### A systematic review of item response theory in language assessment: Implications for the dimensionality of language ability.

- Journal: Studies in Educational Evaluation, 68, 100963.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.stueduc.2020.100963
- URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0191491X2030211X
- Tier: A/B
- Trust notes: Systematic review of 62 IRT-based studies in language assessment.
- LULT use: multidimensionality, skill profiles, caution about single total score.

### International Association for Computerized Adaptive Testing. Introduction to CAT.

- URL: https://iacat.org/introduction-to-cat/
- Tier: B
- Trust notes: Professional association explainer; useful for definitions, not a validation source.
- LULT use: short definition of CAT and pre-calibrated item bank requirement.

### Jang, E. E., & Sawaki, Y. (2025). Advancing language assessment for teaching and learning in the era of the artificial intelligence revolution: Promises and challenges.

- Journal: Language Testing, 42(4), 361-368.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.1177/02655322251348685
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/02655322251348685
- Tier: B
- Trust notes: Editorial framing for AI and language assessment special issue; highlights validity, fairness, reliability, interpretability, privacy, and over-reliance concerns.
- LULT use: AI limitations and governance notes.

### O'Grady, S., Gokturk, N., & Rossi, O. (2026). Artificial Intelligence in Language Assessment Research: A Scoping Review.

- Journal: International Journal of Assessment Tools in Education, 12(2), 317-340.
- DOI: https://doi.org/10.21449/ijate.1602294
- URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/29984475.2026.2633389
